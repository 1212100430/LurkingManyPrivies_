Toilets from "HELL NO" to "HELLO"

Article published Wednesday 4th, Dec by Flamboyant Flamingo

Welcome to Lurking Many Privies (LMP), your Learner-Made, Privacy-focused guide to the best (and worst!) washrooms on campus!

1. The Science Lab's Toilet

Overview
Located in the lab unit next to the Secondary library.
Recently reopened after the lab had been closed for a long period.

Pros:

  • Very clean due to low usage
  • Easy to pee/poop because it’s never crowded
  • Two stalls, same as most other toilets
  • No noticeable smell (neither fragrant nor bad)
  • Always well-stocked with tissues and hand soap

Cons:

  • The unit is quiet and not very populated
  • No one opens the door during lunchtime, so access can be impossible
  • Slightly eerie atmosphere (at least for me) because this toilet is cold.

The toilet is surprisingly clean, mostly because almost no one uses it. With two stalls and a reliable supply of tissues and hand soap, it’s comfortable for both quick stops and longer visits, and there’s no noticeable smell at all. The downside is that the whole unit is pretty empty, and during lunchtime the door is often locked with nobody around to open it. The atmosphere can also feel a bit eerie when it gets too quiet. Still, if you’re the type of person who would skip some crazy experiment just to come and try out a new toilet yourself, this one is definitely worth the detour.

2. The Primary Library's Toilet

Overview
Haven’t really used it until this year
Located inside the primary library

Pros:

  • It’s basically a gamble: sometimes extremely clean, sometimes hit with a pungent smell
  • Weird layout that makes the space feel larger than expected
  • Occasionally has water all over the floor. The temperature is still boiling
  • Not exactly crowded, but somehow there are always people around
  • Never seen any primary kids there - only secondary and high school students

Cons:

  • Two stalls, as usual
  • Often lacks toilet paper
  • The unit is closed during evening recess

The toilet inside the primary library is an unpredictable experience - you never quite know what you’re walking into. Some days it’s spotless; other days a sudden pungent smell greets you at the door. The unusual layout makes it feel more spacious than most campus bathrooms, and with its usual two stalls, it’s functional enough. There’s often water scattered across the floor, and while it isn’t crowded, there always seem to be people passing through. Interestingly, despite being in the primary library, it’s mostly used by secondary and high school students. The biggest drawbacks are the frequent lack of toilet paper and the fact that the unit closes during evening recess, which can be inconvenient. Overall: chaotic but usable.

3. Unit 2.4's Toilet

Overview
Used every morning
Located in Unit 2.4

Pros:

  • Was a terrible toilet last year, but had a real “glow up” this year
  • Very pleasant smell in the morning; the room stays cool (high schoolers might’ve sprayed perfume at some point during the day)
  • Two stalls, same as most other toilets
  • Toilet paper stocked to the absolute brim
  • Back in 2023-ish, there was even a box for period pads, and honestly, I want it back

Cons:

  • Two stalls (as always); the first stall sometimes gets stuck and can’t flush properly
  • Sometimes packed because half the people who go there aren’t actually there to pee
  • Not ideal for pooping due to the crowd

The Unit 2.4 toilet has become part of my daily routine, and it’s had quite a transformation since last year. What used to be just a decent washroom has genuinely glowed up, especially with its surprisingly pleasant morning smell and the cool temperature that makes it way more comfortable than most campus toilets. The toilet paper supply is always overflowing, and I still miss the period pad box they had back in 2023—it deserves a comeback. However, it’s not perfect: the first stall occasionally jams and refuses to flush, and the place can get unexpectedly crowded because not everyone who goes there actually intends to use the toilet. Because of that, it’s not a great spot for pooping, but for morning use, it’s still one of the better options around.

4. The Staircase Toilet

Overview
Located directly under the main big staircase
Accessible only through a nearly abandoned hallway where none of the rooms are in use

Pros:

  • Ideal place for pooping
  • High chance of running into parents in the morning or after school, despite the sign telling them not to enter
  • Hot and humid, but surprisingly still stocked with toilet paper and checked daily
  • Stronger Wi-Fi than most other toilets on campus

Cons:

  • Creepy as hell, especially when you’re alone
  • The people who come here are definitely the ones dealing with constipation or explosive diarrhea
  • Gives you the feeling someone’s watching - the door randomly opens on its own, and the stall barriers are lower than usual
  • You can clearly hear running and movement outside the toilet

The toilet under the main staircase is one of the strangest restroom experiences on campus. Hidden behind a nearly abandoned hallway, it’s weirdly perfect for pooping thanks to its isolation, though that same isolation also makes it extremely creepy when you’re alone. The place is hot and humid, but it’s consistently stocked and checked, which is more than you can say for other toilets. Expect to encounter parents during busy hours, even though there’s a sign telling them not to be there. The Wi-Fi is unusually strong, but the atmosphere is unsettling: the door has a habit of opening by itself, the stalls have lower barriers, and you can hear footsteps and running right outside. It’s the kind of restroom used by people who are either desperate or dealing with catastrophic bowel situations — and honestly, it fits the energy.
