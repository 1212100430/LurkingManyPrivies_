Rubrics & the Grading's criteria

Article published Monday 2th, Dec by Enzyme Lygayse

When evaluating the washrooms across campus, we use a balanced rubric that considers both practicality and student experience. Covers layout, number of booths, spaciousness, ventilation, mirrors, sinks, and dryers. Basically: how well the toilet is built and how pleasant it feels to use.Rates the freshness of the restroom, odor levels, and hygiene. Focuses on whether toilet paper is always available, the consistency of upkeep, repairs, and daily functionality — the most sacred trust between student and stall. From “surprisingly clean” to “I’m holding my breath and my soul.” Includes the vibe of the restroom: student lore, funny legends, and the temperature (freezing, warm, dungeon mode). It’s the personality and energy of the bathroom.
